#STOP-THE-VIOLENCE!

LERATO MASEGO

Lerato Masego survived years of emotional abuse that made her doubt her worth and abilities. After leaving the toxic environment, she began experimenting with homemade lip glosses in her small room using affordable ingredients. Creating beauty products became a form of healing and self-expression. She later turned her hobby into a small cosmetic business that now supports her financially and emotionally. Lerato encourages women experiencing gender-based violence to seek support from trusted people, never stay silent, and believe in their potential. She believes independence begins with self-love, courage, and learning practical skills that can create opportunities and restore confidence after trauma and pain.

NALEDI KGOMOTSO

Naledi Kgomotso survived physical violence in a relationship that left her feeling hopeless and isolated from her family and dreams. After escaping, she focused on rebuilding her life by studying skincare and natural beauty remedies. Mixing creams and body scrubs gave her peace and helped her regain confidence in herself. Today, she owns a growing skincare brand that promotes affordable beauty for everyday women. Naledi encourages survivors to speak up, seek counseling, and never allow fear to define their future. She believes every woman deserves safety, respect, and financial freedom, and that discovering a passion can become the first step toward healing and independence.

AMARA DUBE

Amara Dube survived years of controlling behavior and verbal abuse that made her feel trapped and powerless. Determined to change her life, she began learning makeup artistry through free online tutorials and practiced on friends in her community. Her talent quickly grew, and she started selling affordable cosmetic kits to young women who wanted beauty products within their budget. Through her business, Amara regained her confidence and independence. She now motivates women facing gender-based violence to believe they are stronger than their circumstances. Amara teaches that healing begins when women invest in themselves, discover their talents, and surround themselves with supportive and empowering people

KEITUMETSE RADEBE

Keitumetse Radebe experienced emotional and financial abuse that prevented her from pursuing education and personal growth. After finding the courage to leave, she enrolled in beauty training classes and discovered a passion for cosmetics and skincare. She started a small beauty business offering makeup services and mentoring young girls interested in entrepreneurship. Building her brand helped her recover emotionally while creating a stable income for herself and her family. Keitumetse encourages women to never lose hope, even during painful situations. She believes women can overcome violence by seeking education, learning income-generating skills, and supporting one another through kindness, empowerment, and shared opportunities for growth.

ZINHLE MOYO

Zinhle Moyo survived domestic violence that left her emotionally broken and unsure of her future. During her healing journey, she discovered cosmetic artistry and began creating colorful makeup looks that reflected strength, beauty, and confidence. Makeup became more than a business to her; it became a symbol of reclaiming her voice and identity. She later opened a small cosmetic studio where she empowers women through beauty and self-care workshops. Zinhle inspires survivors to never give up on themselves, no matter how difficult life becomes. She believes women can rise above abuse by embracing their talents, finding supportive communities, and building financial independence through passion and determination.
